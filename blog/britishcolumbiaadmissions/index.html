University of British Columbia admissions

If you're a human seeing this, that's very interesting. This page is only for some custom scripts I have running to explain the admissions of various universities, since their official website can't be read statically.

General US admission requirements

  • Graduation from an academic or college preparation program at a US regionally accredited school. Applicants are encouraged, but not required, to present a minimum of six academic and non-academic Grade 12 courses.
  • English (composition/literature) to the senior level. ESL English courses are not acceptable to satisfy English requirements for admission.
  • Three years of mathematics to the junior level.
  • A minimum grade of B- in an English course to at least the junior level.
  • SAT or ACT. Applicants who have an SAT or ACT test score and would like them to be considered as part of the UBC admissions process are welcome to submit their test scores. Applicants who choose not to submit a standardized test score will not be disadvantaged. (UBC's SAT institution code is 0965 and its ACT institution code is 5259.)
  • AP courses completed as part of the high school curriculum may be used to meet admission requirements.

Typical requirements

Requirements vary. For some, there are no additional requirements. For others:

  • Science degree: 1 credit precalc, 1 credit chemistry, 1 credit physics (or a grade of A in precalc and AP chemistry) are required. Taking language arts, math, and science in your junior and senior year is recommended.
  • International Economics degree: 1 credit precalc is required. Taking language arts, math/science, and social studies in your junior and senior year is recommended.
  • Dental Hygiene: 2 years of biology / AP biology and 2 years of chemistry / AP chemistry are required. A supplemental submission and interview process are necessary. Taking language arts and science in your junior and senior year is recommended.
